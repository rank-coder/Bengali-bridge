# পদাশ্রিত নি‍র্দেশক (Articles)
## Definite Article (Singular & Plural)
- বইটি আমার। {boiṭi amar} [The book is mine] (*1 count*)
- বইগুলো আমার। {boigulo amar} [The books are mine] (*more than 1, but the number is unknown*)
- ঐ কলমটি তোমার। {oi kölomṭi tomar.} [That pen is yours.]
- ওটা তোমার কলম। {oṭa tomar kölom.} [That is your pen.]

> [!NOTE]
> টি {ṭi} is interchangable with টা, খানা, খানি {ṭa, khana, khani}
> গুলো {gulo} is interchangable with গুলি {guli}


## As [[Counter]] or Unit (indefinite)
*টি {ṭi} is a counter when it's right after a number --*
- একটি কলম দাও। {ekṭi kölom dao} [Give (me) a pen] (*1 count, but don't know which one. i.e. indefinite*)
- দশটি বই আছে আমার। {döshṭi boi ache amar} [Ten books, I have] (*more than 1, but don't know which ones. i.e. indefinite*)

> [!NOTE]
> here টি {ṭi} is interchangable with টা, খানা, খানি {ṭa, khana, khani}

### Omitted টি {ṭi}
*For large numbers, it's better to ommit the counter টি {ṭi} --*
- পাঁচশো কলম লাগবে আমার। {pashsho (pacsho) kölom lagbe amar.} [I need/want 500 pens.]
*Sometimes for stylistic effect --*
- সাত সমুদ্র, তেরো নদী। {shat shomudro, tëro nodi} [The seven seas, and thirteen rivers]
- এক দেশে ছিল এক রাজা। {ëk deshe chilo ëk raja} [(Once) there was a king in a country]

## As [[Counter]] and Definite Article Simultaneously
*Reverse the order to make it definite --*
- হ্যাঁ, বই দুটি আছে। {hë, boi duṭi ache} [Yes, (we) have the 2 books.]
- kölom duṭi lal. [The 2 pens are red.]
- duṭi lal kölom. [2 red pens.]
- duṭi kölom dao. [Give me 2 pens.]
- kölom duṭi dao. [Give me the two pens.]

*Sometimes even reversing the order is not necessary. The context makes it obvious. It can be considered stylistic --*
- সোমবার দুই দেশের রাষ্ট্রপতি বৈঠক করেন। {shombar dui desher rashṭropoti boiṭhok kören} [On Monday, the presidents of the two countries held a meeting.]
