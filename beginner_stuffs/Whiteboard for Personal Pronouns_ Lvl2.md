# 2nd person
## Neutral Honour
### Subject Pronoun
tumi [You]
tumi ke? [Who are you?]
tumi jao [You go]
tumi ëkjon shikkhök. [You are a teacher]
### Possessives
tomar [Your/Yours]
tomar boi. [Your book/ Your books.]
ei böiṭi tomar. [This book is yours.]
### Object
tomake [You]
ami tomake cini. [I know you.]
## Low Honour
### Subject
tui [You]
tui ke? [Who are you?]
tui ja. [You go.]
### Possessives
tor [Your/Yours]
tor boi [Your book]
ei boiṭi tor. [This book is yours.]
### Object
toke [You]
ami toke cini. [I know you.]

## High Honour
### Subject
apni [You]
apni ke? [Who are you?]
apni jan. [You go.]
### Possessives
apnar [Your]
apnar boi. [Your book]
ei boiṭi apnar. [This book is yours]
### Object
apnake [You]
ami apnake cini. [I know/recognize you.]

# 3rd Person
## Neutral
### Subject
she [He/She]
she ëkjon bhalo manush. [He/She is a good person.]
she bëbsha köre. [He/She does business.]
she baṛi jacche. [He/She is going home.]
### Possessives
tar [his/her]/[his/hers]
tar ekṭi gaṛi ache. [He/She has a car.]
tar boi [His/Her book]
ei boiṭi tar. [This book is his/hers.]
ei gaṛiṭi tar. [This car is his/hers.]
### Object
take [Him/Her]
ami take cini. [I know him/her.]
(tumi) take ḍako. [You call him/her.]
## High Honour
### Subject
tini [He/She]
tini ëkjon shikkhok. [He/She is a teacher.]
tini bëbsha kören. [He/She does business.]
tini ḍhaka jacchen. [He/She is going to Dhaka.]
tini baṛi jacchen. [He/She is going home.]
### Possessives
tãr [his/her]/ [his/hers]
tãr ekṭi gaṛi ache. [He/She has a car.]
tãr boi [His/Her book]
ei boiṭi tãr. [This book is his/hers.]
ei gaṛiṭi tãr. [This car is his/hers.]
### Object
tãke [Him/Her]
ami tãke cini. [I know him/her.] (Statement.)
(tumi) tãke ḍako. [You call him/her.] (Command)
